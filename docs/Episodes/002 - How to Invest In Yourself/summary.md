# How to Invest In Yourself

> 📄 [View Full Transcript](transcript.md)

**YouTube:** [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=C1QYF5WYzCo)

## Overview

Leo argues that the most important distinction in life is between short-term and long-term investing — and that most people default to the short-term mindset in every domain: career, relationships, fitness, and personal development. The video makes the case that investing in yourself — your skills, your mind, your inner game — is the highest-return activity you can undertake. Using a personal SEO story (where he framed every backlink as potentially worth thousands of dollars), Leo shows how your mental model of value determines how much effort you put in. The deepest insight is that abstract inner qualities — emotional stability, confidence, creativity — compound across every area of life and are worth far more than any specific skill.

## Key Insights

*   **Short-Term vs. Long-Term Investment Mindset:** Dabbling and jumping between opportunities creates short-term investment patterns that never compound. Sustained commitment to one direction is what builds the skill base that generates disproportionate returns.
*   **Building Career Capital (Cal Newport):** In skill-based careers, what you're actually building is career capital — the accumulated expertise that makes you more valuable than competitors. This is the primary asset, not connections or luck.
*   **Skills = Value You Provide Others:** Whether employed or running a business, you earn money because you provide value. Building skills is the direct mechanism for increasing the value you can offer. More skill = more leverage = higher income.
*   **Hard Skills vs. Soft Skills:** Technical skills (painting, programming, design) are necessary but often not sufficient. Soft skills — people skills, humor, business acumen, the ability to sell — frequently have higher ROI because they multiply the effect of hard skills.
*   **Inner Game > Outer Game:** The abstract inner qualities — emotional stability, confidence, creativity, peace — are the deepest investments. They compound across every life domain simultaneously: career, relationships, focus, resilience. A single investment in emotional stability pays dividends for decades.
*   **Reframe the Value of Each Increment:** Leo's SEO story: when you mentally value every link at $1,000 (because 2,000 links could produce a site earning $200,000/year), you build twice as many links as someone who sees them as worthless. How you think about each unit of effort determines how much effort you exert.
*   **Behavioral Change Alone Backfires:** Trying to change behavior without changing the underlying thinking produces temporary change followed by relapse. Real transformation requires changing how you think — your beliefs, mental models, and self-concept.

## Core Concepts

*   **Career Capital:** The accumulated skill and expertise that makes you uniquely valuable in your field. Builds slowly through consistent long-term investment and cannot be acquired through shortcuts.
*   **Inner Game:** The psychological layer beneath behavior — beliefs, identity, emotional patterns, self-concept. The most leveraged place to invest because it shapes all action automatically.
*   **Outer Game:** The specific technical skills and behaviors visible to others. Necessary but insufficient without a strong inner game foundation.
*   **Value Exchange:** The fundamental economic reality — you receive from others in proportion to the value you provide them. Increasing your skills directly increases the value of this exchange.

## Practical Takeaways

*   Audit where your time goes — are you investing in skills that compound or consuming things that don't build value?
*   Pick one career domain and go deep rather than spreading across many areas superficially.
*   Calculate the long-term value of each skill-building session — reframe the ROI to increase your motivation to do it.
*   Invest in soft skills (people skills, communication, emotional intelligence) — they multiply the value of all technical skills.
*   Prioritize inner game work: emotional stability, confidence, and creativity compound across every life domain.
*   Recognize that sustainable change requires changing how you think, not just what you do.
