# Why Life Coaching Works

> 📄 [View Full Transcript](transcript.md)

**YouTube:** [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8cbtMhHpLC8)

## Overview

Leo explains the mechanics of why life coaching works — and why it produces different results than therapy, consulting, or mentoring. The distinctive feature of coaching is that coaches ask questions rather than give advice, trusting that clients already have the answers within themselves. Through 200–300 hours of coaching training (IPEC) and 90–100 hours of live coaching experience, Leo discovered that the most powerful coaching moments occur when a well-timed question forces a client to pause and explore a branch of thinking they had never considered. The root cause of most stuck patterns is not lack of knowledge but inner blocks — specific beliefs, assumptions, and interpretations that filter how someone perceives their options. Coaching externalizes and challenges these blocks in a way that generic self-help cannot.

## Key Insights

*   **Coaches Ask, Not Tell:** Unlike consultants (who give specific advice) or mentors (who share experience), coaches ask questions and trust the client to arrive at their own answers. This counterintuitive approach is more powerful because the client's specific inner blocks require specific solutions.
*   **You're Stuck in Your Own Story:** Most people are trapped in the same branching thought tree, exploring only the paths they've always explored. An effective question sends you down a branch you've never considered — producing genuine insight rather than reshuffling existing beliefs.
*   **The Real Block is Not Knowledge:** If a person isn't going to the gym, it's almost never because they don't know they should go. It's because of specific inner resistance — fear of looking incompetent in public, guilt about taking time away from family, a belief that effort is vain. The block is psychological, not informational.
*   **Blocks Are Specific to Each Person:** Two people both failing to exercise may have completely different inner blocks. Generic advice addresses neither. Coaching identifies and addresses the specific psychological obstruction unique to each individual.
*   **The Most Powerful Coaching Move:** When a client pauses after a question, struggles to answer, or says "I don't know" — that's the moment of maximum coaching value. The silence means they're exploring genuinely new cognitive territory.
*   **Coaching Complements, Not Replaces, Self-Help:** Books, seminars, and programs give a solid foundation. Coaching addresses what those cannot: the personal, idiosyncratic inner blocks that prevent the generic advice from being implemented.
*   **Think Like a Coach with Yourself:** Even without a coach, you can apply coaching logic: identify what you actually want (not what you think you should want), then ask what's really stopping you (not the surface reason, but the belief underneath it).

## Core Concepts

*   **Life Coaching:** A questioning-based approach to personal development that trusts clients to find their own solutions. Distinct from therapy (pathology-focused) and consulting (expert-advice-focused). Effective for taking already-functional people to higher levels.
*   **Inner Blocks:** Specific beliefs, past experiences, assumptions, and emotional patterns that filter how a person sees their options and generates resistance to change.
*   **The Coaching Question:** A question that sends the client down an unexplored branch of thinking, producing genuine insight. Identified by the pause or silence it creates.
*   **Inner Game:** The psychological foundation from which all behavior emerges. Changing inner game is more sustainable than changing behavior directly.

## Practical Takeaways

*   When you're stuck on a problem, ask: what am I assuming that I haven't examined? What branch of thinking have I never explored?
*   Identify the specific inner resistance blocking you — not "I should go to the gym" but "what exactly is the mental picture that makes me not want to go?"
*   Generic advice (books, podcasts, seminars) is for building the foundation. Use coaching for the specific personal blocks that generic tools can't reach.
*   The best questions create a pause — in your own journaling or reflection, look for questions that genuinely stump you. Those are the most valuable.
*   Behavior change without inner game change produces temporary results. Start with the belief that drives the behavior.
