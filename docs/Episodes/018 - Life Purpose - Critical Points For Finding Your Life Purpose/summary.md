## Overview

This video explores life purpose as a dynamic, evolving process rather than a static destination. Leo argues that without a clear purpose, people drift through life unfulfilled and mediocre, spending a third of their time working on something that doesn't align with their deepest values. He uses the "onion model" to explain how purpose develops through layers of self-discovery over years and decades.

## Key Insights

- **Life purpose is not fixed:** Purpose evolves continuously throughout your life—it's not something you discover once and commit to forever. It requires constant reassessment and "peeling back layers."

- **Purpose is deeply connected to career:** Since you spend roughly one-third of your life working, aligning your career with your values and purpose is essential for fulfillment.

- **Most people lack purpose by default:** People are not born knowing their purpose; it must be actively built through introspection, exploration, and deliberate action. Assuming others naturally have purpose is deceptive.

- **Without purpose, you become a leaf in the wind:** Without grounding your life in purpose, you're easily swayed by external motivators (higher pay, prestige, benefits) and never authentically express yourself.

- **Deeper layers reveal authentic expression:** As you peel away outer layers of purpose, you move toward expressing your core values and having the specific impact on the world you truly desire.

- **Alignment requires courage and action:** Figuring out your purpose is one challenge; aligning your actual life with it requires significant courage, sacrifice, and potentially years of strategic effort.

- **Purpose is about contribution, not just consumption:** Fulfillment comes not from accumulating possessions or experiences, but from creating value and having a meaningful impact on others.

- **Testing and iteration are essential:** You cannot plan your entire life's purpose in advance—you must actively experiment, try different paths, and make course corrections based on what actually resonates with you.

## Core Concepts

**The Onion Model of Purpose:** Life purpose operates like an onion with multiple layers. You start with outer layers (basic career intuitions), then progressively peel them back to discover deeper layers (authentic values, specific impact, core identity). This is a dynamic process that continues throughout life.

**Authenticity Through Alignment:** True fulfillment comes when your work allows you to express your complete authentic self—not just parts of yourself constrained by corporate structures, clients, or special interests that undermine your creative vision.

**Purpose as a Long-Term Pursuit:** Building and aligning your life with purpose is a multi-year or multi-decade project. It requires patience, persistence, and willingness to make major life changes (leaving jobs, starting businesses, changing careers).

**The Meta-Purpose Approach:** If you don't currently have a purpose, make your immediate purpose the act of *finding* your purpose. This gives you direction and a framework for exploring different paths.

**Impact-Based Definition:** Purpose is fundamentally about the impact you want to have on others and the world—not about personal gain, status, or consumption. The question is: what legacy do you want to leave?

## Practical Takeaways

- **Reflect on introspective questions:** Ask yourself: What would I do if money weren't a factor? What accomplishments make me most proud? What kind of impact do I want to have? What legacy do I want to leave?

- **Identify patterns of fulfillment:** Look back at moments when you felt most fulfilled and proud. These moments often point toward your authentic purpose and what you'd want to pursue full-time.

- **Make finding your purpose your current purpose:** If you're lost, don't wait for clarity—make actively searching for and testing your purpose your immediate objective. Read, research, explore, and test different fields.

- **Experiment and test different paths:** Don't try to plan everything perfectly. Actively try different work, projects, and career paths to discover what resonates with you. You'll learn more from doing than from thinking alone.

- **Plan long-term but iterate dynamically:** While setting a long-term vision, stay flexible and willing to course-correct as you learn more about yourself. Your purpose will evolve, and that's healthy.

- **Work with a coach or mentor:** If you're struggling to clarify your purpose, working with a life coach can help you gain clarity and create an actionable plan to move from your current situation toward your ideal career and impact.

- **Distinguish between means and ends:** Be honest about whether your current work is truly aligned with your purpose or if you're using it as a means to an end. If it's the latter, create a deliberate plan to transition toward what you actually want.
