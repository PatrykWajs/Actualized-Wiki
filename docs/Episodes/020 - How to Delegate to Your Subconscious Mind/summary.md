# How to Delegate to Your Subconscious Mind

## Overview

This video explores how to transition skills and behaviors from your conscious mind to your subconscious mind through deliberate repetition. Leo argues that mastery in any domain—whether public speaking, fitness, career, or personal development—requires understanding that most of what makes you effective operates at the subconscious level, and that consistent repetition is the mechanism for embedding conscious intentions into automatic, effortless subconscious performance.

## Key Insights

- **The Illusion of Conscious Control**: Most of what you accomplish in life stems from your subconscious mind, which has been programmed through years of repetition and conditioning. The belief that everything is consciously willed is largely an illusion.

- **Repetition as the Core Mechanism**: Anything repeated consistently will eventually "bubble down" from conscious awareness into subconscious habit. This is the fundamental process for skill development and mastery.

- **Two Layers of Performance**: All performance has a baseline subconscious layer (ingrained habits, body language, vocal patterns) and a conscious intentional layer (specific goals you're working toward in that moment). Both are necessary.

- **Conscious Mind Bottleneck**: The conscious mind can only handle a limited number of things simultaneously. Trying to consciously control too many aspects of performance creates mental overload and degrades results.

- **Flow State Through Automation**: Elite athletes perform at their best when fully in flow—not thinking about execution. This is only possible because thousands of repetitions have automated the skills to the subconscious level.

- **Delegation Reduces Stress**: Once you trust the delegation process and commit to repetition, you can release the pressure of trying to consciously control everything. This reduces anxiety and improves performance.

- **Ubiquity of Subconscious Processing**: Walking, talking, breathing, maintaining balance, heart function—all the basic competencies you developed as a child are now subconscious and effortless. This demonstrates the power of the system.

- **Conscious Focus as a Precious Resource**: Your conscious awareness is limited and valuable. The key to success is directing it toward creativity, improvement, and what you genuinely want to build—not toward worry, fear, or frivolous thoughts.

## Core Concepts

**The Conscious-Subconscious Transfer Process**: The mechanism by which deliberately practiced conscious skills become automatic subconscious abilities through consistent repetition over time. This is the pathway to mastery.

**Baseline vs. Intentional Performance**: The unconscious foundation of skills and behaviors you've internalized (baseline), plus the conscious goals and improvements you're currently working on (intentional). Both layers operate during any performance.

**The Delegation Principle**: Rather than trying to consciously manage all aspects of a skill, intentionally delegate the mechanical execution to your subconscious through repetition, freeing conscious resources for higher-level focus.

**Flow State**: A state of effortless, optimal performance that occurs when skills are so thoroughly automated they operate at the subconscious level without conscious interference.

**Repetition Drilling**: The brute-force but reliable process of repeating an action or practice many times until it becomes instinctual and automatic—like memorizing multiplication tables or phone numbers.

## Practical Takeaways

- **Assess Your Repetition**: For any skill you want to master, ask yourself: Am I doing it repeatedly? Daily? Weekly? Ensure your practice frequency matches the skill development timeline required.

- **Focus on One Conscious Element at a Time**: Rather than trying to improve multiple aspects simultaneously (vocal tonality, body language, movement, content), pick one element to consciously work on while trusting other skills stay on autopilot.

- **Trust the Delegation Process**: Once you commit to consistent repetition, release the pressure of perfect execution. Trust that skills will bubble down to the subconscious naturally.

- **Use Deliberate Drills**: Like athletes, employ structured drills focused on specific skill components. Repetition through drills is more effective than casual practice.

- **Apply to Any Domain**: Whether it's public speaking, coaching, fitness, business, or socializing, use the repetition principle. Don't expect mastery without consistent practice over time.

- **Redirect Conscious Focus Productively**: Audit where you're directing your limited conscious awareness. Redirect it from worry, gossip, and anxiety toward creativity, learning, and building the life you want.

- **Monitor the Baseline Shift**: Pay attention to what aspects of a skill are becoming automatic over time. As skills delegate to the subconscious, new conscious intentions become available to work on.
