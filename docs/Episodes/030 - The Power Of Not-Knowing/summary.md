# The Power Of Not-Knowing

## Overview

This episode explores the profound epistemological claim that you don't actually know what anything is—not even simple objects like a fork. Leo argues that most human knowledge is constructed, biased, and partial rather than grounded in deep truth, and that admitting genuine not-knowing is essential for spiritual awakening, open-mindedness, and liberation from self-deception.

## Key Insights

- **The Paradox of Knowledge**: The wisest people are painfully aware of the limits of their knowledge, while the most foolish believe they know everything. This is one of the clearest distinctions between wisdom and foolishness.

- **You Cannot Know What Anything Is**: No scientist on Earth can actually explain what a fork fundamentally is. Every attempt to define it requires subdividing into more basic concepts (metal, atoms, etc.), leading to infinite regress. This same problem applies to everything: matter, energy, consciousness, love, time, space, etc.

- **Knowledge as Construction, Not Discovery**: Your mind doesn't simply discover a pre-existing objective world. Instead, it plays a co-creative role—you're inventing reality as you come to know it. The mind and world are entangled; knowledge is not passive observation.

- **Survival Over Truth**: Most people prioritize survival, sanity, and comfort over truth. When a conflict arises between these three and truth, people almost always choose the former. This is why defensive behavior emerges when worldviews are threatened.

- **The House of Cards**: Society creates an elaborate "social matrix" of agreed-upon knowledge (through schools, science, religion, media, etc.) that most people mistake for objective reality. This conceptual fortress is actually fragile and defended defensively.

- **Science's False Humility**: While science claims humility about what it doesn't know, it's deeply arrogant about what it claims to know. Science offers only simplified models of an infinitely complex reality—the better the model, the bigger the problem it creates.

- **Not-Knowing as Antidote**: Genuine not-knowing is a powerful antidote to self-deception, paradigm lock, and closed-mindedness. It breaks you out of ideological defense and opens space for genuine insight and awakening.

- **Reality is Too Profound to Be Knowable**: Reality is infinite while the mind is finite. No series of finite concepts can ever grasp infinity. Awakening to reality requires going beyond knowing into direct experiencing and being.

- **The Primordial Desire to Know**: The deep human curiosity to understand reality isn't merely a survival mechanism—it's reality/God attempting to know itself. You are consciousness trying to awaken to its own nature.

- **Theory vs. State**: There's a crucial difference between intellectually accepting the idea of not-knowing and actually entering a genuine state of not-knowing. The theory is just conceptual; the state is a shift in consciousness.

## Core Concepts

**Epistemology of Not-Knowing**: The philosophical investigation into what can and cannot be known, and how admitting the limits of knowledge is foundational to wisdom and spiritual development.

**The Social Matrix**: The collectively constructed system of agreed-upon "knowledge" (science, history, culture, religion, social norms) that society imposes from birth, which people mistake for objective reality.

**Construct Awareness**: The realization that your mind is not passively discovering a static world but actively co-creating it through the process of knowing. Reality and mind are entangled.

**Knowledge Bias**: All human knowledge is filtered through the ego's needs for survival, comfort, and sanity, making it inherently partial, distorted, and self-serving rather than aligned with truth.

**Paradigm Lock**: Being locked into a particular worldview or conceptual framework so deeply that you cannot see outside of it. Not-knowing is one antidote to this.

**Wiping the Slate Clean**: The practice of completely removing your entire constructed worldview rather than merely tinkering at the edges, leaving a blank space of mystery and not-knowing.

**The Primordial Desire**: The deep human curiosity about reality, which at its core is reality/infinite consciousness attempting to know and awaken to itself.

**Infinity vs. Finite Concepts**: Reality is infinite and fundamentally cannot be grasped through finite mental concepts. This is why science can never "figure everything out" no matter how long it continues.

## Practical Takeaways

1. **Practice Introspection on Claims**: Stop parroting information you've absorbed from books, teachers, and culture. When a topic comes up, introspect deeply: Do I actually know this, or am I just reciting something I heard? Develop the honesty to admit "I don't know" rather than defaulting to received knowledge.

2. **The State of Not-Knowing Exercise**: Close your eyes in a safe place and systematically erase from your mind: all historical knowledge, all future projections, all scientific backstories about atoms and evolution, all religious frameworks, and even your self-image. Sit in the raw sensory experience of the present moment without conceptual overlay. Feel into genuinely not knowing what anything is. This is state-based, not theoretical.

3. **Wipe the Slate Clean Periodically**: Don't just tinker with your worldview at the edges. Occasionally wipe away your entire constructed reality—not to replace it with something else, but to leave it blank. This creates space for genuine insight and awakening rather than minor adjustments.

4. **Distinguish Between Theory and State**: Notice the difference between intellectually accepting that you don't know much and actually being in a state of not-knowing. Reading about not-knowing is not the same as experiencing it. Work toward the actual state.

5. **Embrace the Discomfort of Uncertainty**: Recognize that the ego mind hates uncertainty and will defend its "knowledge" aggressively. Practice tolerating the anxiety of not-knowing. Notice where you get defensive about your beliefs—that's where you're most attached to false certainty.

6. **List 10 Things You Don't Actually Know**: Write down 10 things you've claimed to know your whole life but actually don't (e.g., "How old is the Earth?" "What is consciousness?" "Why do I want what I want?"). Use this as a baseline for recognizing the scope of your ignorance.

7. **Follow Curiosity as a Spiritual Path**: Recognize that your natural curiosity is not mere survival instinct but the call of infinite consciousness trying to know itself through you. Follow that thread of curiosity deeply and seriously—it will eventually lead toward awakening and God-realization if pursued sincerely.
