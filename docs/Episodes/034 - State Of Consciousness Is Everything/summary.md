## Overview

Leo presents a foundational principle of his consciousness teachings: that state of consciousness is everything, and all of reality consists entirely of states of consciousness. He argues that reality is composed of "figments of consciousness" rather than physical matter, and that understanding this requires a paradigm shift from bottom-up materialist thinking to top-down consciousness-based understanding. This is not mere philosophy but a direct experiential insight accessible through deep awakening and contemplation.

## Key Insights

- **Nothing exists outside consciousness**: All objects, including physical reality itself, exist within and as states of consciousness. There is no material substrate independent of consciousness.

- **Figments vs. atoms**: Reality is not built from elemental particles upward, but rather consciousness holistically imagines entire figments (objects, people, experiences) from the top down. A Snickers bar, Albert Einstein, or New York City are all equally figments of consciousness—not reducible to atoms.

- **The sanity-reality trade-off**: Your current experience of a solid, material reality is actually a highly coherent state of consciousness. To truly perceive reality as figments of consciousness would require losing conventional sanity, as the consistency that feels like materiality would unravel.

- **Infinite figments, no hierarchy**: No figment is more fundamental than another. A sexist joke, a pina colada, and an electron are equally valid figments of consciousness with equal complexity and equal ease for consciousness to imagine. Scale and simplicity are illusions.

- **Consciousness is absolute and irreducible**: Consciousness cannot be reduced to smaller components. It is infinite, unbounded, and self-identical. Any attempt to ground consciousness in a substrate (atoms, neurons, code, etc.) merely creates additional figments.

- **Intelligence requires top-down causation**: Only mind/consciousness can be truly intelligent and creative. Material systems cannot generate genuine intelligence, sentience, or creativity—these capacities require top-down consciousness, not bottom-up computation.

- **State determines everything**: Your entire experience—thoughts, emotions, perceptions, memories, future—is determined by your current state of consciousness. Change your state, and reality changes completely.

- **Infinite subdivision and recursion**: Any figment can be infinitely subdivided into smaller figments, like fractals. You can zoom into New York City infinitely, finding more detail and figments, but it's all one holistic creation of consciousness.

## Core Concepts

**Figments of Consciousness**: The fundamental building blocks of reality. Not physical objects, but qualitative, holistic elements imagined by consciousness. Everything from atoms to people to entire cities are figments.

**State of Consciousness**: The overall configuration or mode of consciousness at any given moment. States are fluid, infinite, and determine all experience. Examples include dreaming, waking, intoxicated, sick, emotional, angry, or horny states.

**Top-Down vs. Bottom-Up Creation**: Consciousness creates reality holistically from the top down (imagining whole figments), not by assembling pieces bottom-up like legos. This is opposite to the materialist paradigm.

**Absolute vs. Relative**: All figments are absolute—they are exactly what they are, self-identical. There is no "more real" substrate beneath them. The attempt to ground figments in more fundamental figments is infinite regress.

**Coherence as Materiality**: The sense that reality is solid, material, and physical is actually the coherence and consistency of consciousness's figment-weaving. Insanity occurs when this coherence breaks down.

**Infinite Mind**: Another name for consciousness—infinite, boundless, eternally creative, capable of sentience, intelligence, and comprehension. It is the only truly irreducible reality.

**Scale Invariance**: Consciousness has no preferred scale. It can create something as small as an electron or as large as a galaxy with equal ease. Size is relative and itself a figment.

## Practical Takeaways

- **Hold an object and contemplate it as a figment**: Take your smartphone, hold it, and try to directly grasp that it is not made of atoms but is a figment of consciousness. This is not intellectual but experiential.

- **Notice your state of consciousness changes throughout the day**: Become aware of how your state shifts when you're tired, angry, sick, excited, or intoxicated. Notice how the same event feels completely different depending on your state.

- **Question the assumption that reality is material**: Rather than accepting materialist explanations, ask yourself: Why must reality be made of atoms? Why can't it be made of the actual things I perceive?

- **Meditate on infinite subdivisibility**: Pick any object and contemplate how it could infinitely contain smaller details and figments, like a fractal zoom. This trains the mind to see consciousness's creative recursion.

- **Use psychedelics to experience non-coherent states**: Leo suggests that psychedelics can help break the coherence of your normal state of consciousness, giving you a direct experience of how the apparent solidity of reality depends on state.

- **Grasp the relativity of substrate**: Recognize that claiming atoms are more "fundamental" than pina coladas or jokes is arbitrary. All figments have equal metaphysical status.

- **Prepare for the loss of sanity involved in full awakening**: Understand that truly waking up to this truth requires going somewhat "insane" by conventional standards, so approach this work with patience and self-compassion.
