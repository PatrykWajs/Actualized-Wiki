## Core Concepts

**Heavy Metal Toxicity**
The accumulation of lead, mercury, and arsenic in organs and brain tissue from environmental and dietary sources, causing neurotoxicity that manifests as cognitive decline, mood disorders, fatigue, and autoimmune conditions over years or decades.

**Chelation**
The chemical binding of heavy metals to compounds like DMSA (dimercaptosuccinic acid) and ALA (alpha lipoic acid), which form complexes that allow the body to excrete metals through urine; requires precise timing and dosing protocols.

**DMSA (Dimercaptosuccinic Acid)**
A FDA-approved oral chelator that binds lead and mercury in the bloodstream and organs but cannot cross the blood-brain barrier; dosed at ~30 mg/kg/day divided into 3-4 hour intervals.

**Alpha Lipoic Acid (ALA)**
A unique chelator that crosses the blood-brain barrier, making it essential for detoxifying heavy metals from neural tissue; causes more severe detox symptoms and requires gradual dose escalation (25-250 mg every 3 hours).

**Provoked Urine Test**
A diagnostic test where a large DMSA dose is consumed and all urine is collected for 3-6 hours, then analyzed to measure how much heavy metal is being excreted—the gold standard for detecting toxicity without false negatives from blood tests.

**Half-Life Protocol**
The critical principle that DMSA (4-hour half-life) and ALA (3-hour half-life) must be redosed before the previous dose is fully eliminated, preventing dangerous redistribution of mobilized metals into organs between doses.

**Detox Symptoms/Herxheimer Response**
Temporary worsening of symptoms during chelation as mobilized heavy metals circulate and cause neurological irritation; distinguishes the detox effect from actual harm and typically improves with continued chelation.
