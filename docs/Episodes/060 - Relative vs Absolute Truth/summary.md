## Overview

This foundational episode explores the critical distinction between relative and absolute truth—a distinction Leo argues most people fail to make, leading to numerous philosophical traps. The video opens the possibility that absolute truth exists and is directly accessible to human consciousness, challenging materialist and relativist narratives that have pervaded modern culture.

## Key Insights

- **Relative Truth Requires Division**: All relative truths operate by dividing unified reality into multiple parts and comparing them against each other. Examples include "the sky is blue" (relative to human perception, Earth's atmosphere, time of day) and velocity (which only has meaning relative to a reference frame).

- **Absolute Truth is Non-Dual**: Absolute truth operates in the non-dual domain—it has no opposite, cannot be compared to anything, and cannot be subdivided by the mind. It is singular, sovereign, and self-knowing.

- **The Veil of Perception is a Misconception**: Rather than truth being behind a veil we cannot pierce, Leo suggests considering that appearance IS reality—that what you experience directly IS absolute truth, with no medium between consciousness and what is true.

- **Existence as Absolute Truth**: Your own existence is absolute truth that requires no external validation. Only you can know that you exist; no scientist, authority figure, or other being can prove your existence to you—you must know it directly through being.

- **Authority and Sovereignty**: The chief delusion is outsourcing your authority to external validators (gurus, scientists, government, media). True access to absolute truth requires reclaiming your complete sovereignty and trusting your own direct experience.

- **Science Operates Entirely in the Relative Domain**: Scientific measurements (meters, seconds, joules) are all relative divisions of reality. Scientists don't actually know what these fundamental units ARE—they only know how to compare and measure them.

- **The Mind Must Be Opened First**: Understanding absolute truth intellectually is different from genuinely opening your mind to its possibility. Leo emphasizes this requires active, conscious engagement—not passive thinking.

- **Validation Cannot Work in the Absolute Domain**: Absolute truth cannot be validated by external observers or repeated experiments because validation itself requires duality (two observers, two experiments). The non-dual nature of absolute truth makes external validation impossible and unnecessary.

## Core Concepts

**Relative Truth**: Truths that depend on context, comparison, division, and perspective. They require at least two parts of reality to be compared against each other. All scientific measurements, cultural values, and everyday statements exist in this domain.

**Absolute Truth**: That which is true under all circumstances with no context or conditions. It is singular, non-dual, cannot be compared to anything, and knows itself simply by being itself. It exists independent of any observer's interpretation.

**Non-Duality**: The state of undivided wholeness without opposites. Absolute truth cannot have an opposite (like relative truth has relativistic pairs). It is completely unified and sovereign.

**The Veil of Perception**: A materialist assumption that consciousness is separated from reality by a veil of perception. Leo challenges this by suggesting that appearance/experience IS reality, with no medium between them.

**Sovereignty**: Complete authority over one's own reality and validation. Leo argues humans must reclaim sovereign consciousness rather than outsourcing authority to external validators.

**Dualism**: The framework of operating with opposites, comparisons, and divisions. All relative truth operates dualistically; absolute truth is non-dual.

## Practical Takeaways

- **Open Your Mind to Possibility**: Before seeking absolute truth, consciously work to open your mind to the possibility that it exists and is accessible. This requires moving beyond intellectual agreement to genuine mental openness—pause and do this actively.

- **Examine Your Own Existence**: Become directly conscious of your own existence right now. Look at your hand, feel your presence. Notice that you know you exist with absolute certainty, not through external validation but through direct self-recognition. This is your direct access to absolute truth.

- **Question External Validation**: Notice where you've outsourced your authority. What beliefs do you hold because a guru, scientist, politician, or media figure told you to hold them? Begin reclaiming your own sovereignty.

- **Distinguish Relative from Absolute Questions**: When asking questions about reality, notice whether you're asking relativistic questions (big or small? good or bad? useful or harmful?) that collapse absolute truth into the relative domain.

- **Practice Direct Experience**: Rather than debating absolute truth intellectually, commit to practices of direct experience and consciousness exploration to validate these ideas through your own awareness.
