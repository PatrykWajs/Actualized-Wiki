## Core Concepts

**Social Construction vs. Physical Reality**
The distinction between something being "real" (physically measurable) versus "constructed" (mentally created). Gender is physically real in biological differences but constructed in how we categorize and assign meaning to those differences. Construction doesn't mean fake or changeable at will—it means created by minds for practical purposes.

**Meta-Scientific vs. Scientific**
Science measures and compares properties (chromosomes, genitals, hormones) but cannot define abstract categories like "man" or "woman." Category-creation is meta-scientific—it precedes and enables science. Science cannot prove what something *is*, only measure its properties.

**Identity as Survival Mechanism**
All identities (gender, human, professional) are mental constructs the mind creates to navigate survival in specific environments. They're functional illusions—useful but limited, and they carry implicit webs of meaning and behavioral expectations you barely consciously recognize.

**Arbitrariness of Boundaries**
Boundaries between categories (man/woman, human/animal, you/your mother) are not given in nature but drawn by minds for practical purposes. Where you draw the line (at skin? at genetics? at reproductive capacity?) is a choice, not a discovery.

**Psychological Investment and Defensiveness**
The intensity of people's reactions to gender questioning reveals how deeply their survival needs are tied to gender identity. Defending gender categories is defending one's sense of self, sexuality, social belonging, and reproductive future.

**Cultural Relativism of Gender Norms**
What constitutes appropriate masculine/feminine appearance, behavior, occupation, and role varies dramatically across cultures and history, indicating these standards are culturally constructed rather than biologically determined.
