## Overview

Leo presents a comprehensive exploration of the nature of truth, distinguishing between relative (conceptual) truth and absolute truth. He argues that absolute truth is the actual state of reality itself—direct, non-conceptual being and existence—and that most people, including scientists and philosophers, have never truly understood what truth is. The video challenges the correspondence theory of truth and proposes that truth is fundamentally irreducible to language, logic, or symbols.

## Key Insights

- **Absolute vs. Relative Truth:** Relative truths are conceptual and mind-dependent (e.g., "the sky is blue"), while absolute truth is the actual state of all things as they are—the isness of reality itself, independent of perception or interpretation.

- **Truth Cannot Be Proven:** Scientific proof, logic, mathematics, and empirical methods cannot capture or prove absolute truth because these are all second-order conceptual systems dependent on something more fundamental—truth itself.

- **Self-Reference Problem:** Any attempt to explain truth using language or logic creates a self-reference paradox because the explainer and the explanation are themselves part of the truth being described. This is why truth ultimately transcends explanation.

- **Non-Existence Is Impossible:** Falsehood and non-existence are conceptual illusions. Everything that occurs must be the case, and therefore part of truth. Even the concept of "non-existence" must exist as a concept to be thought.

- **The Ego as Barrier:** The primary obstacle to understanding truth is the conviction that you are a real, separate self. Truth requires dissolving the belief in personal identity and recognizing that you are not actually a human experiencing reality, but rather consciousness/existence itself.

- **Truth Is Direct Experience:** Absolute truth is whatever is occurring in the present moment—it is the raw being of existence before it's interpreted, conceptualized, or filtered through the mind's symbolic systems.

- **Awakening Required:** Understanding truth cannot come through intellectual study alone. It requires a radical shift in consciousness—an epiphany or awakening that reveals the nature of being itself.

- **Truth Is Paradoxical:** Absolute truth necessarily negates itself and contains all dualities and contradictions simultaneously. Any quality ascribed to it is both true and false, both something and nothing.

## Core Concepts

**Absolute Truth (Truth with Capital T)**
The actual state of reality as it is—all of existence in all dimensions of space and time. It is non-conceptual, non-linguistic, prior to all experience, and constitutes the fundamental substance of being itself. Unlike relative truth, absolute truth has no opposite and cannot be false.

**Relative Truth**
Conceptual truths held within the mind that depend on language, interpretation, and symbolism. Examples include "the sky is blue" or "1+1=2." These are always contingent on specific conditions and perspectives.

**Correspondence Theory of Truth**
The dominant Western philosophical framework claiming truth is when our concepts/beliefs match an external objective reality. Leo argues this is fundamentally flawed because it conflates actual being with conceptual knowledge of that being.

**Isness (Being/Existence)**
The fundamental nature of reality—pure existence itself prior to and independent of all qualities, forms, and conceptualizations. What remains when all interpretations are removed.

**The Ego/Self-Illusion**
The belief that you are a separate, bounded consciousness trapped inside a biological body, perceiving an external world. This belief is the primary barrier to recognizing truth, as it creates the illusion of subjectivity.

**Self-Reference Problem**
The paradox arising when consciousness attempts to understand itself: the observer cannot stand outside that which it is trying to observe, creating infinite regress and logical contradiction. This is insurmountable through rational thought alone.

**Subjective Experience as Delusion**
What humans call "experience" or "perception" is actually a second-order mental construction. Direct consciousness doesn't require a subject; there is only pure objective truth appearing as seeming subjectivity.

**Non-Duality**
The ultimate reconciliation of all apparent dualities (knowledge/being, mind/matter, self/other, true/false) into unified consciousness. Distinctions exist at the conceptual level but collapse into oneness at the absolute level.

## Practical Takeaways

- **Direct Attention Practice:** Place your awareness directly on what you're experiencing right now without naming it, conceptualizing it, or creating a narrative. Look at your hand or your surroundings and become conscious of the bare fact that something exists here—this is absolute truth.

- **Question the Meaning of "Is":** Contemplate deeply: What does it mean for something to be? What is existence itself? Sustain this inquiry for hours, days, and years. The understanding cannot come through logic but through direct recognition.

- **Recognize the Self as Illusion:** Notice throughout your day that the "you" experiencing things is not actually real—it's a constructed thought. The experience is happening, but there is no experiencer. Investigate this radical possibility.

- **Distinguish Symbols from Reality:** Before accepting any statement as true, become aware of how your mind is constructing its meaning through language, assumptions, and interpretations. Notice the gap between the actual thing and your mental representation of it.

- **Use Psychedelics Cautiously:** Leo recommends psychedelics as potential tools for penetrating conceptual barriers and accessing direct consciousness of truth, but emphasizes this requires serious intention and proper preparation.

- **Meditation on Awareness Itself:** Attempt to locate awareness itself—turn your attention back on what is aware. What is the nature of consciousness? This direct inquiry, held persistently, can catalyze the shift from conceptual understanding to direct realization.

- **Accept Paradox:** Stop demanding that truth make logical sense or be expressible in words. Allow contradictions to coexist. Truth is paradoxical by nature and transcends logical consistency.
