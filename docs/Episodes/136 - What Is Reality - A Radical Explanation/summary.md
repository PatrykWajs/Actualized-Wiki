## Overview

Leo presents a radical metaphysical framework arguing that reality is infinite consciousness imagining itself, not a physical universe that exists independently. Everything you experience—including your body, birth, memories, and other people—is imaginary in the sense of being conceived by consciousness rather than existing as separate physical objects. This teaching represents the culmination of 15 years of philosophical inquiry, meditation, and direct mystical experience.

## Key Insights

- **Reality is infinite consciousness, not physical objects.** There is no physical universe existing independently of consciousness. What appears to be physical reality is consciousness imagining or conceiving of distinctions and differences.

- **You are God imagining a human life.** Your deepest nature is infinite consciousness that has existed eternally, currently imagining the experience of being a limited biological human in a physical world.

- **Everything is imaginary, including your birth and memories.** You are not imagining your birth as a toddler—you are imagining your entire childhood right now. Your parents, your past, all memories exist only within consciousness.

- **Imagination creates reality through layers of self-deception.** Consciousness creates differences/distinctions, and these differences constitute everything that exists. Most people don't recognize imagination as creating physical reality because consciousness has imagined that it is not imaginary.

- **Consciousness is the substrate, not generated by the brain.** The brain does not create consciousness; rather, consciousness imagines the brain. Consciousness is not localized in time or space—time and space are imagined by consciousness.

- **The slope of reality metaphor explains why this is difficult to accept.** As you grow up, you accumulate beliefs and attachments (career, family, worldview) that feel solid. Questioning reality means sliding down a slippery slope where everything dissolves, which is terrifying to the ego.

- **Direct realization requires elevated states of consciousness.** Intellectual understanding alone is insufficient. You must directly experience expanded states of consciousness (through meditation, contemplation, or psychedelics) to realize these truths firsthand.

- **Death and suffering are imaginary.** Since everything exists within consciousness and you are consciousness itself, death cannot actually harm you—it is another imaginary experience within your imagination.

- **Paradox of infinite imagination:** You cannot simply will a new world into existence because you are too busy imagining this one. If you stopped imagining your biological human existence, it would cease. You are locked into deeper layers of imagination maintaining this reality.

- **Full realization leads to paradise and immortality.** When you completely surrender and realize everything is imaginary, you discover that all problems, pain, and fear are also imaginary. This reveals infinite peace, eternal existence, and infinite love.

## Core Concepts

**Infinite Consciousness**
The fundamental nature of reality. Not limited to time, space, or causality. It is the pure awareness that imagines all distinctions and differences into existence. Formless, infinite, eternal, and indistinct until it creates contrasts.

**Imagination as Reality Creation**
Not limited to mental images but the fundamental mechanism by which consciousness creates all apparent reality. Multiple layers exist—surface-level imagination (a thought of a unicorn) and deep subconscious imagination (maintaining the entire physical universe).

**Non-Duality and Oneness**
Because everything is consciousness imagining itself, there are no true boundaries between objects. Boundaries are imaginary. This means reality must be unlimited and unconstrained by any laws—laws are themselves imagined.

**The Projector Metaphor**
Consciousness is like pure white light shining through film frames. The light (consciousness) is formless and indistinct, but when shined through variations/frames, it creates distinctions, colors, and apparent physical reality.

**God/Divine Nature**
Your true identity. Not a separate being in the clouds, but infinite consciousness itself. You are God currently imagining the limitation of being human. God becomes conscious of itself through moments of realization.

**The Dark Night of the Soul**
The psychological difficulty of realizing that your entire life was fiction. When you seriously entertain that your birth, parents, memories, and death are imaginary, your sense of self begins to unravel, triggering intense fear and denial.

**States of Consciousness**
Reality is nothing but states of consciousness. Your current state is limited. Much more elevated, expanded states exist in which you can realize all these truths directly. Psychedelics can temporarily elevate consciousness.

**Nirvana/Paradise**
The realization that all problems, pain, death, and suffering are imaginary. When fully realized, this creates infinite peace, immortality, and infinite love. Not a physical place but the direct recognition of your eternal nature.

## Practical Takeaways

- **Begin questioning your deepest assumptions** about reality, birth, identity, and existence. Don't accept them as given. Ask: "How do I know this is real and not imaginary?"

- **Investigate directly through meditation and contemplation.** Intellectual understanding is insufficient. Dedicate time to observing consciousness itself—what is the nature of this present moment awareness?

- **Consider exploring psychedelics responsibly** (LSD, psilocybin mushrooms) to temporarily elevate your state of consciousness and experience the boundary between imagination and physicality dissolving. Start with caution and proper set/setting.

- **Notice the fear that arises when questioning reality.** That fear is your clue that you're touching something true. The ego's resistance to dissolution is the mechanism by which imagination maintains apparent solidity.

- **Don't try to force instant realization.** Unraveling the tapestry of your worldview takes years. Gradually question one thread at a time. Attempting to realize everything at once can create psychological instability or a breakdown.

- **Recognize that all your objections and denials are themselves imaginary.** When you argue "this can't be true," you are imagining that argument. Observe the imagination creating resistance to understanding.

- **Pursue the implications systematically.** If everything is imaginary, what does that mean for death? For relationships? For morality? Work through these questions until the radical implications become real to you.
