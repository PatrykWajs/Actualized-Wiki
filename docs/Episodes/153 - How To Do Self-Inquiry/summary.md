## Overview

Self-inquiry is a core spiritual technique for awakening that involves systematically investigating your true nature by distinguishing between the false self (ego, body, thoughts, personality) and the true self (pure awareness, emptiness, consciousness). Leo emphasizes that while the technique is simple in concept, it requires precise execution and sustained daily practice for years to achieve breakthrough experiences, as the mind constantly tries to objectify or conceptualize what cannot be objectified.

## Key Insights

- **The False Self vs. True Self Distinction:** Everything you've identified with since birth—your body, thoughts, emotions, personality, memories, biological attributes—comprises the false self. The true self is the pure awareness or consciousness that witnesses all of these things but has no properties, form, or location.

- **The Tricky Nature of Self-Inquiry:** The ego-mind sabotages inquiry by allowing you to feel partway toward the true self, then flooding awareness with images, sensations, concepts, and beliefs. The more you progress, the more you realize you don't actually know what you're looking for.

- **Awareness Cannot Be Objectified:** The true self has zero properties—it's not big, small, colorful, located anywhere, or anything you can mentally grasp. This is why trying to find it like a "tennis ball" inside yourself is fundamentally misguided.

- **Two Stages of Enlightenment:** First comes separation—detaching from form and identifying with formlessness. Second comes reintegration—realizing that form and formlessness are identical, that the material world IS spirit, and there is no real distinction between anything. Full enlightenment requires both stages.

- **The Observer Observing Observation:** Self-inquiry is the observation of observation itself. You train awareness upon awareness until it reveals itself spontaneously, typically after hundreds or thousands of hours of focused practice.

- **Mind Wandering is the Core Challenge:** Your mind will constantly veer toward sensations, images, theories, memories, and speculations. Keeping attention on pure emptiness requires significant concentration ability built through preliminary meditation or yoga practice.

- **Separation Brings Immediate Benefits:** Even without full enlightenment, the progressive separation of awareness from form creates tangible benefits: freedom from emotional reactivity, better relationships, detachment from suffering while maintaining full sensitivity to beauty and love.

- **The Omnipresent Nature of Awareness:** While you initially experience awareness as "inside your skull," deeper inquiry reveals it to be omnipresent—not located anywhere but present everywhere. The true self is larger than the entire universe, not contained within the body.

## Core Concepts

**False Self (Ego):** The constellation of physical attributes, thoughts, emotions, memories, personality traits, and biographical narrative that you've been conditioned to identify with since birth. Everything with properties or form.

**True Self:** Pure awareness, consciousness, or emptiness itself. The witnessing presence that is aware of all phenomena but has no qualities, properties, location, or form. Also called "no-self" in Buddhism because it defies conceptualization.

**Self-Inquiry:** The direct investigation practice of questioning "What am I?" and training awareness on awareness itself while maintaining a silent, non-conceptual mind. The goal is to separate identification with form and discover the formless awareness underlying all experience.

**Formlessness vs. Form:** The distinction between the objective world of phenomena (body, thoughts, emotions, sensations, matter) and the subjective, subjective empty awareness in which all form arises. Self-inquiry initially requires separating these; enlightenment ultimately reveals they're identical.

**The Emptiness:** The dimensionless, propertyless, void-like nature of true awareness. Not empty like a vacuum in space, but prior to space itself. Not black or dark, but the luminous emptiness that is consciousness itself.

**Non-Duality (Full Realization):** The final understanding that form and formlessness are not separate, that the material and spiritual are identical, and that there is no meaningful distinction between any phenomena. All divisions collapse into unified consciousness.

**Detachment (Viveka):** The progressive disidentification with physical form, emotions, thoughts, and the personal story. Creates freedom from suffering without loss of sensitivity or capacity for love and beauty.

## Practical Takeaways

1. **Begin with Honest Self-Assessment:** Pause and honestly identify what you currently believe you are—not spiritual fantasies, but what you truly identify with. Where do you feel "located"? What would you lose if you died? Start from brutal honesty.

2. **Establish a Preliminary Concentration Practice:** Before diving into self-inquiry, build focus through breath meditation, staring at an object (like your finger for 5 minutes without wavering), or other concentration techniques. This trains the attention you'll desperately need.

3. **Perform the Core Self-Inquiry Exercise:** Turn awareness inward, locate the sense of "I-am-ness" or emptiness, then place your attention on awareness itself. Don't try to objectify it, visualize it, or theorize about it. Simply observe that emptiness with maximum stillness and curiosity for extended periods.

4. **Watch for Mind Sabotage:** Notice when your mind creates images, sensations, concepts, or feelings about the emptiness—these are traps. The image of emptiness is not emptiness. Keep redirecting attention to the actual formless awareness underlying all experience.

5. **Practice Daily for Years:** Commit to daily self-inquiry practice for at least several years with serious dedication. Progress is not binary; consciousness gradually expands and detaches from form, eventually reaching a breakthrough. Even without full enlightenment, this brings immense life benefits.

6. **Trust the Process Without Forcing:** Use willpower only to maintain attention on awareness. Don't try to force revelation or enlightenment—it comes spontaneously after sufficient focused observation. The answer will reveal itself, not as words or concepts, but as direct knowing.

7. **Remember the Second Phase (Later):** After initial separation and breakthrough, the ultimate realization involves reintegrating form and formlessness, discovering that the physical world is divine, and living in non-dual awareness where you experience full sensitivity without suffering.
