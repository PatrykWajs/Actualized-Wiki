## Core Concepts

**Observer-Dependent Reality**: In quantum mechanics, there is no objective reality independent of observation. The act of measurement collapses the wave function and determines what appears. This demolishes the classical materialist notion of a reality existing independently "out there."

**Epistemological Crisis**: Quantum mechanics revealed that the very process of knowing is inseparable from what is known. The knower, the knowing process, and the known are not three separate things but aspects of one whole—creating an unsolvable problem for purely rationalist approaches.

**Holism vs. Atomism**: Reality is fundamentally undivided and interconnected (holistic), not composed of independent atomic building blocks that can be understood in isolation. Fragmentation is a function of the mind's analytical process, not reality itself.

**Mind-Stuff Foundation**: Physical reality is ultimately made of "mind-stuff"—consciousness or awareness is the ground of all existence. What we call matter is derivative, secondary, and ultimately a mental construct or idea within universal consciousness.

**The Grid of Interpretation**: The mind projects categories, distinctions, and frameworks (the "grid") onto reality. We then mistake this projection for reality itself. Science and perception always involve interpretation—there is no raw, uninterpreted access to reality from within the dualistic mind.

**Cosmic Religious Feeling**: A direct, non-conceptual experience of oneself as inseparable from the whole universe. This is not belief in dogmas or deities but a felt sense of interconnectedness that transcends the ego and personal desires.

**Post-Rational Thinking**: Going beyond rationality while retaining its insights—accessing intuition, infinite intelligence, and direct perception that logic alone cannot reach. This is not anti-rational but trans-rational.

**Non-Duality**: The ultimate insight that the apparent separation between observer and observed, subject and object, self and world is illusory. In truth, there is only one undivided being appearing as multiplicity.
