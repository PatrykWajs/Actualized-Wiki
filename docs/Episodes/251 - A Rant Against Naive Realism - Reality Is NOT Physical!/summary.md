## Core Concepts

**Naive Realism (Physicalism/Materialism)**
The assumption that reality is an objective, three-dimensional physical space containing independent objects that exist whether or not anyone perceives them. This is humanity's default worldview but is philosophically incoherent and scientifically obsolete.

**The Phenomenological Field**
The totality of direct sensory experience—colors, sounds, sensations, thoughts. This is all consciousness ever directly accesses. All concepts about external reality are mental constructs within this field, not access to something beyond it.

**The "Behind the Scenes" Problem**
The unfounded assumption that there's an objective physical world existing independently of perception. Leo argues this creates infinite regress and should be questioned as a foundational metaphysical assumption.

**Quantum Fields**
Modern physics reveals reality is composed of non-physical quantum fields with values distributed infinitely—not solid particles or matter. This completely contradicts naive realism but the implications haven't reached mainstream understanding.

**The Cosmic Turtle Myth**
An ancient metaphor where reality rests on turtles all the way down—illustrating how explanations that posit hidden foundations don't actually solve anything. Modern science commits the same error with the Big Bang.

**Non-Physical Mysticism**
The recognition that reality is fundamentally mysterious, non-mechanistic, and non-rational. It cannot be reduced to material causes and effects; it simply IS—which is why it's indistinguishable from magic.

**Paradigm Undermining**
The systematic process of questioning and eroding your deepest metaphysical assumptions about reality through investigation, skepticism, and contemplation—not to adopt new beliefs, but to free the mind from filters.
