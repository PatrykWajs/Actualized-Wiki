## Overview

This video continues Leo's exploration of the "no self" doctrine by establishing the foundational distinctions needed to empirically investigate the nature of self, reality, and existence. Rather than offering philosophical theories, Leo guides viewers through direct questioning to challenge assumptions about identity, encouraging an empirical investigation into who we really are beyond concepts, language, and the constructed self-image.

## Key Insights

- **The Self is Conceptual, Not Real**: What you believe to be your "self" is actually a collection of concepts, labels, and stories created by the internal voice—not an actual entity. Your self-image is as fictional as imagining yourself on a beach sipping a piña colada.

- **The Voice Creates Reality**: Your internal monologue continuously constructs a web of concepts that you mistake for reality. This voice creates the "I" label and then convinces you that this label is real, building a self-sustaining system of illusion.

- **Perception vs. Concept**: There are distinct levels—the thing itself (noumenon), sensations/perceptions (phenomena), and concepts overlaid on those perceptions. You live almost entirely in the conceptual domain without realizing it.

- **The Inner/Outer World is a Concept**: The distinction between inner subjective experience and outer objective reality is itself a concept. When this concept dissolves, the inner and outer merge into a unified reality.

- **Perceiver/Perception is a False Dichotomy**: The assumption that there must be a perceiver to perceive something doesn't logically hold up under scrutiny. This distinction is another concept that can be deconstructed.

- **Reality is Not Limited by Intuition**: Human intuition evolved for survival, not for understanding the nature of existence. We should not assume reality must conform to our limited intuitions—existence itself is already miraculous and counterintuitive.

- **The Investigation is Empirical, Not Theoretical**: Unlike philosophy, this is about direct first-person observation and investigation. You must look for yourself rather than adopt theories or beliefs about who you are.

## Core Concepts

**Noumenon vs. Phenomenon (Kant's Distinction)**
The thing-in-itself versus our sensory perceptions of it. We can know phenomena but may assume we cannot access the noumenon; however, this framework itself can be transcended.

**Matter vs. Qualia**
Matter/energy are the physical substrates, while qualia are the subjective experiences (the redness of red, the taste of orange). The hard problem is understanding what qualia actually are.

**Inner vs. Outer World**
The conventional split between subjective inner experience and objective external reality. This distinction is conceptual and can be dissolved through investigation.

**Conceptualization**
The process by which the voice creates abstract mental representations, labels, stories, and self-images that we mistake for direct reality. This is the primary mechanism of illusion.

**The Voice**
The internal dialogue and mental imagery that continuously constructs your sense of self, reality, and meaning. It operates through both words and images, creating thoughts that build concepts.

**Self-Image**
The conceptual construct of who you believe you are—your personality, identity, appearance, abilities, and characteristics. This is entirely created by the voice and is not your actual nature.

**The False Self**
The entire constructed identity built on the voice's continuous labeling and storytelling. It includes your sense of being a distinct entity separate from reality.

**The True Self (Real You)**
What remains when all concepts, labels, and the self-image are dissolved. Leo suggests this is not a "self" in the conventional sense but rather your actual being-ness merged with reality.

## Practical Takeaways

- **Begin Questioning Assumptions About Identity**: Don't accept the obvious statement "I am my body." Start investigating what part of you actually feels threatened when criticized, injured, or humiliated. What is being threatened?

- **Observe Your Internal Voice**: Become aware of the constant internal dialogue throughout your day. Notice how it creates labels (I am this, I am that), tells stories, and then believes its own narratives.

- **Investigate the Self-Image Directly**: Ask yourself: Do I have a mental image of myself? Where is it coming from? Can I observe it? Notice how you can imagine yourself on a beach or recall an image of the back of your head—these are concepts, not reality.

- **Question the Perceiver/Perception Distinction**: Rather than accepting that you must be perceiving the orange, investigate: Who is perceiving me? Who is seeing the seer? Trace this recursion to its end in actual experience, not theory.

- **Suspend Your Intuitions About What's "Possible"**: Practice being open to counterintuitive possibilities about reality. Recognize that your intuitions evolved for hunting and mating, not understanding metaphysical reality.

- **Recognize Your Level of Ignorance**: Genuinely acknowledge that you don't actually know who you are, what reality is, or what existence is. This honest uncertainty is the beginning of genuine inquiry.

- **Commit to Empirical Investigation Over Theory**: Resist the urge to generate new theories or beliefs. Instead, commit to looking directly at your experience. The answer to "What am I?" can only come from direct observation.
