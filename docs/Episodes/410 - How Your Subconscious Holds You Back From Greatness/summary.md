# How Your Subconscious Holds You Back From Greatness

## Overview

Leo explores how deep subconscious programming from childhood creates subtle limiting beliefs that hold us back from achieving our goals and living our full potential. Through a personal anecdote about picking up a mushroom off the floor, he reveals how surface-level behaviors often mask deeper psychological issues—in his case, scarcity mindset around money that unconsciously sabotages his business decisions and life purpose.

## Key Insights

- **Scarcity mindset manifests in subtle ways**: Leo's resistance to throwing away a mushroom wasn't about hygiene but revealed deep financial insecurity from childhood that still influences his decisions despite being financially secure.

- **Childhood programming runs deep**: Beliefs formed in childhood and early adulthood become embedded in the subconscious and continue to operate automatically, even when objectively contradicted by current circumstances.

- **Security enables risk-taking**: Drawing from psychological research on infant attachment, Leo explains that we can only venture into our potential and take risks when we feel fundamentally secure.

- **Money psychology blocks life purpose**: His financial hang-ups prevented him from pursuing his true calling for years because he couldn't separate passion from profit concerns.

- **Opposing money problems**: Some people waste money everywhere; others (like Leo) are overly conservative and stingy, both of which are limiting patterns that need addressing.

- **Subconscious holds are identifiable in everyday behavior**: By paying attention to automatic reactions (like hesitating before throwing away food), you can trace back to root causes in your past.

- **All limiting beliefs are workable**: No matter how deep or severe the original trauma or belief, there are tools and techniques available to reprogram and release these blocks.

- **Active work is required**: Simply understanding the problem intellectually isn't enough; deliberate personal development work over months or years is necessary to truly reprogram deep patterns.

## Core Concepts

**Scarcity Mindset**: A psychological pattern where someone feels there's never enough (money, resources, safety), leading to hoarding, overly conservative behavior, and inability to invest in themselves or opportunities—even when objectively secure.

**Subconscious Limiting Beliefs**: Deep programming from childhood experiences that operate automatically below conscious awareness, creating behavioral patterns that contradict current goals and circumstances.

**Childhood Wounds as Life Blocks**: Negative experiences or messages received in childhood (being called stupid, ugly, or hearing family money conflicts) become foundational beliefs that limit exploration, risk-taking, and growth.

**Security as Foundation for Growth**: The psychological principle that safety and security are prerequisites for healthy exploration, learning, and taking risks—just as infants explore more when mothers are present.

**Wealth Psychology**: The study of limiting beliefs, triggers, and emotional patterns people hold around money, beyond just financial mechanics.

## Practical Takeaways

- **Notice subtle automatic behaviors**: Observe your unconscious reactions in daily life (food waste, money decisions, relationship patterns) as they often point to deeper limiting beliefs.

- **Trace behaviors back to origins**: When you notice a pattern that doesn't serve you, investigate childhood experiences or messages that might have created it.

- **Create new rules consciously**: Leo implemented a rule to always throw food away rather than picking it up, using conscious action to reprogram automatic responses.

- **Study money psychology specifically**: If you suspect financial hang-ups are blocking you, actively seek out resources on wealth psychology, limiting beliefs about money, and reprogramming exercises.

- **Invest in your own goals**: Identify where you might be too stingy or conservative with investing in yourself, your business, or your life purpose due to scarcity fears.

- **Commit to long-term personal development work**: Recognize that reprogramming deep subconscious patterns takes sustained effort over months or years, not quick fixes.

- **Use available resources**: Access books, exercises, and techniques specifically designed for the area where you're blocked (money, relationships, health, social anxiety, etc.).
