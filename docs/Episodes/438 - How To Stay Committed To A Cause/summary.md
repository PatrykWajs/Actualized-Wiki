# How To Stay Committed To A Cause

## Overview

Leo explores what true commitment really means by breaking down an abstract concept into tangible, practical action. Using his personal example of buying batteries for his microphone equipment, he demonstrates how commitment manifests in real-world decisions and how to push through resistance when your mind questions your dedication to your life purpose.

## Key Insights

- **Commitment is tangible, not abstract** — True commitment reveals itself through concrete actions and decisions. Buying 60 batteries instead of hesitating over a smaller pack is commitment made visible.

- **Your subconscious mind resists commitment** — Even when you consciously declare commitment to something, your subconscious may still resist due to limiting beliefs about scarcity, uncertainty, and keeping options open.

- **Recommitment is continuous** — You don't make a commitment once; you must continually recommit. The initial excitement fades, challenges arise, and your mind tries to pull you back.

- **Motivation naturally fluctuates** — Even when pursuing your perfect life purpose, motivation dips and you'll feel demotivated. This is normal and expected, not a sign you chose wrong.

- **Your mind tricks you during low points** — When motivation drops, limiting beliefs resurface and your mind whispers that you should quit. Recognizing this as a mental pattern allows you to push through.

- **Leaving options open undermines commitment** — While flexibility has its place, genuine commitment requires making clear cuts and saying "I'm all-in." Hedging your bets signals lack of real commitment.

- **Abstract principles require personal application** — Wisdom only transforms your life when you interpret and apply it to your specific circumstances. There's no one-size-fits-all implementation.

- **Wisdom becomes powerful through tangible experience** — When you see abstract principles working in real life (like the battery example), you develop deep appreciation and buy into self-mastery.

## Core Concepts

**Commitment as Action** — Commitment isn't a feeling or declaration; it's demonstrated through decisions and resource allocation. Where you spend your money, time, and effort reveals your true commitments.

**The Subconscious-Conscious Gap** — Your conscious mind may declare commitment while your subconscious resists through hesitation, doubt, and rationalization. Bootstrapping yourself means gradually aligning these levels.

**The Commitment Cycle** — Initial excitement → difficulty/resistance → temptation to quit → recommitment → breakthrough. This cycle repeats throughout any long-term endeavor.

**Mind as Resistance** — Your mind is programmed for comfort and homeostasis. It will deploy limiting beliefs, worst-case scenarios, and reasonable-sounding objections to keep you from committing to growth.

**Practical Wisdom** — Principles only matter if they translate to tangible improvements in your life. Abstract wisdom becomes powerful when you see it working in concrete situations.

## Practical Takeaways

- **Find your "batteries"** — Identify a specific, tangible way to demonstrate commitment in your life purpose or goals. Stock up on the resources (physical or otherwise) that signal to yourself and the universe that you're all-in.

- **Notice resistance and push through it** — When you feel hesitation around committing to your goals, pause and recognize it as your mind trying to keep you comfortable. Acknowledge it and act despite it.

- **Make clear cuts, not hedges** — Instead of keeping multiple options open, pick your commitment and go all-in. The psychological power of total commitment outweighs the safety of keeping escape routes.

- **Recommit regularly** — Don't rely on initial excitement. Build a practice of regularly recommitting to your life purpose, especially during low motivation periods.

- **Apply principles to your own life** — Take abstract wisdom and translate it into specific, meaningful actions unique to your situation. Don't wait for perfect instructions; use your best judgment.

- **Track commitment through resource allocation** — Monitor where your money, time, and attention go. These reveal your true commitments more honestly than your words.

- **Expect and normalize motivation fluctuations** — Understand that dips in motivation are part of the process, not evidence that you've chosen wrong. Plan to push through these valleys.
