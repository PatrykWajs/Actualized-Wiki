## Overview

This video addresses the fundamental question of life purpose and how to discover what you should do with your life. Leo argues that finding your life purpose requires creating a compelling vision for yourself, overcoming the fear and limiting beliefs that prevent you from dreaming big, and taking action to turn that vision into reality.

## Key Insights

- **Purpose is personal, not predetermined.** Purpose is not god-given or fixed—it's whatever you decide is valuable and worth your effort and commitment.

- **Fear and limiting beliefs are the primary obstacles.** Most people don't engage with their life purpose because they fear the unknown and doubt their ability to achieve big dreams, so they self-limit before even trying.

- **Current circumstances don't determine your potential.** Your past, your present situation (debt, health issues, location, social status), and your perceived limitations are not accurate predictors of what you can accomplish in 5-10 years.

- **You unconsciously suppress your biggest dreams.** Many people have a "layup dream" they're willing to share, but an even bigger dream they won't even admit to themselves because it feels too impossible or scary.

- **Motivation requires vision.** You lack motivation not because you're lazy but because you don't have something big enough to motivate you. A compelling vision generates the energy needed for action.

- **The how will reveal itself through action.** You don't need to know the exact path or mechanism to achieve your vision—once you have clarity on what you want, the process of working toward it will open unforeseen avenues.

- **Faith in potential is essential.** You must trust that potential exists for you the same way your current position would have seemed impossible 5-10 years ago, yet you're here now.

## Core Concepts

**Life Purpose** — Your personal definition of what would be valuable and worthwhile to dedicate your energy toward. It's the contribution or creation you can make that would improve the world in your eyes.

**The Invisible Glass Wall** — The psychological barrier created by fear and limiting beliefs that prevents you from articulating and committing to your biggest dreams, even though you may secretly hold them.

**Creator Mindset** — Seeing yourself not as a consumer or passive participant in life, but as an artist or creator capable of producing something meaningful that contributes to the world.

**Vision-Before-Action Framework** — The principle that you must first create a clear, compelling vision of your future self and what you want to create, and from that vision, motivation and action naturally follow.

## Practical Takeaways

- **Disconnect and visualize.** Turn off all devices (phone, computer, television, internet) and spend time alone asking yourself: "What is the most compelling vision for my future self that I could see?"

- **Project far into the future.** Imagine yourself 5-10 years from now rather than next month. This gives your mind enough conceptual space to overcome the fear that it needs to happen immediately.

- **Write down your biggest dream.** Don't self-censor or filter—articulate even the dream that seems impossible or too outrageous. Get it out of your head and onto paper.

- **Focus on your contribution.** Ask yourself: "What could I create or produce that would improve the world in my eyes?" Think beyond clichéd charity work to something genuinely meaningful you'd be proud to create.

- **Separate vision from mechanics.** Don't reject your dream because you can't see how to achieve it. Trust that once you're motivated and taking action toward your vision, the "how" will gradually reveal itself.

- **Recognize your self-imposed limits.** Examine where you're telling yourself something is "not realistic" or "not possible for me." Question whether this belief is actually true or if you're selling yourself short based on past circumstances.
