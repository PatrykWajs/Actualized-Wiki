## Overview

This video explores the subconscious mind's critical role in shaping your life results, behaviors, and success. Leo explains that approximately 95% of daily actions operate at a subconscious level and that lasting personal change requires retraining the subconscious through consistent, repeated stimulus rather than one-time conscious efforts.

## Key Insights

- The subconscious mind controls roughly 95% of your daily behaviors, from habitual routines to deep-seated beliefs about yourself and reality
- Permanent change happens at the subconscious level, not the conscious level—one-time motivation or knowledge is insufficient for lasting transformation
- The subconscious operates like an animal mind, responding to "carrots and sticks" (incentives and consequences) through repetition rather than intellectual understanding
- Your self-image, beliefs about your capabilities, and your model of reality are all subconscious and directly limit or enable your success
- Successful people achieve results by either systematically visualizing inspiring visions and reinforcing them, or through persistent failure until breakthroughs occur
- Consistency and persistence are the hallmark traits of successful people because they continuously retrain their subconscious through repeated stimulus
- Once habits are imprinted into your subconscious, they operate automatically without depleting willpower, freeing conscious energy for other pursuits

## Core Concepts

**Conscious vs. Subconscious Mind**
The conscious mind is your awareness and deliberate thinking, while the subconscious mind operates below awareness and controls habits, beliefs, self-image, values, and your model of reality.

**Subconscious as "Animal Mind"**
The subconscious operates instinctually and responds to incentives (carrots) and consequences (sticks) through repetition, similar to how animals learn, rather than through intellectual reasoning.

**The 95% Rule**
Approximately 95% of your daily actions—from brushing teeth to having conversations—are executed subconsciously without conscious deliberation or awareness.

**Retraining Through Repetition**
Meaningful change requires repeated stimulus over time. One-time conscious inputs (reading, motivation, therapy sessions) are insufficient; transformation requires consistent reinforcement of new patterns.

**Positive vs. Negative Pattern Etching**
Deep-seated negative habits and limiting beliefs can only be replaced through equally deep-seated positive habits and empowering thoughts that are continuously reinforced.

**Willpower Optimization**
Rather than relying on limited daily willpower, successful self-development involves using conscious effort strategically to imprint grooves in the subconscious so it handles the "heavy lifting" automatically.

## Practical Takeaways

- **Develop awareness**: Throughout your day, notice activities you're performing automatically and recognize they're being controlled by your subconscious mind
- **Assess alignment**: Evaluate whether your subconscious mind is currently aligned with or working against your conscious goals and desires
- **Use visualization**: Create inspiring visions of your desired outcomes and drill them into yourself repeatedly through visualization to reshape your subconscious beliefs
- **Establish deep-seated positive habits**: Rather than relying on motivation or willpower, build deeply ingrained positive thought patterns and behavioral habits through consistent repetition
- **Practice journaling, affirmations, and introspection**: Use these techniques to surface unconscious patterns and intentionally imprint new beliefs into your subconscious
- **Embrace persistence**: Understand that meaningful change takes time and repeated reinforcement—commit to consistent practice even when results aren't immediately visible
- **Strategic willpower allocation**: Use your limited daily willpower intelligently to create subconscious grooves that then operate automatically without ongoing conscious effort
