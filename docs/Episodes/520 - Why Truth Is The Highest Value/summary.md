# Why Truth Is The Highest Value

## Overview

This episode presents the radical thesis that truth must be your highest value in life, above all other values including love, family, happiness, and social justice. Leo argues that when people prioritize anything above truth—money, success, power, relationships, or even noble causes—they inevitably fall into self-deception, corruption, and a progressively fake existence. He demonstrates this through extensive examples of how various misaligned values corrupt entire lives and societies.

## Key Insights

- **People do not actually care about truth**, despite believing they do. Everyone regards their current worldview as true while remaining unconscious of their self-deception. This is the foundation for understanding all human dysfunction.

- **Caring about truth is an attitude and lifestyle choice**, not simply holding correct beliefs. It means having a genuinely curious, open mind on a truth-seeking journey rather than defending pre-existing answers or worldviews.

- **Prioritizing anything above truth guarantees self-deception and corruption**. This is not conditional—it's a certainty. If you don't place truth first, your mind will fabricate whatever it needs to justify your other priorities.

- **Corruption is fundamentally the non-prioritization of truth**. Political corruption, corporate malfeasance, toxic relationships, and personal dysfunction all trace back to valuing something other than truth above truth itself.

- **All other values become corrupted when placed above truth**. Money becomes fake, relationships become toxic, spirituality becomes false, activism becomes dogmatic—whatever you place above truth inevitably becomes inauthentic and hollow.

- **Truth is ontologically more fundamental than any other value**. Reality itself cannot be subordinate to feelings, money, or any other consideration. Truth is the necessary precondition for existence and for anything to have value at all.

- **Truth is the only value God/existence would organize around**. A universe built on maximizing pleasure, money, sex, or any specific outcome would collapse into inconsistency. Only truth provides the stable foundation for a coherent universe.

- **Your actual values are revealed by your life choices, not your stated beliefs**. Where you spend time and money, who you associate with, and what you compromise on—these reveal your real value system versus your fantasy of what you value.

## Core Concepts

**The Two-Value Problem**: Everyone has a fantasy of what they value and an actual embodied value system revealed by their choices. Real change requires aligning these through truth-seeking.

**Corruption as Value Inversion**: Corruption isn't just taking bribes; it's systematically prioritizing anything above truth. It metastasizes through institutions and psyches progressively over time.

**Truth vs. Content**: Caring about truth is different from defending specific beliefs, ideologies, religions, or scientific theories. It's about having an attitude of genuine inquiry rather than defending answers.

**Reality as Foundation**: Existence itself is truth. You cannot have a universe without truth, but you can have a universe without feelings, money, or any specific value. This makes truth categorically different from other values.

**The Slippery Slope of Misaligned Values**: When you prioritize something other than truth, your mind must lie to itself to resolve cognitive dissonance. Each lie compounds, creating a snowball effect of increasing self-deception and inauthenticity.

**Feminine vs. Masculine Orientation**: While truth-seeking is sometimes coded as masculine and feeling as feminine, Leo argues truth must be primary regardless of gender. All feminine values (love, compassion, community) become corrupted if not grounded in truth.

## Practical Takeaways

- **Audit your actual values by observing your behavior** for one week. Track where you spend time, money, and energy. This reveals your real value hierarchy, not your fantasy version. Use this honest assessment as a starting point.

- **Practice truth-seeking as a lifestyle attitude**: When facing a decision or problem, pause and ask "What is actually true here?" rather than "What do I want to believe?" or "What should I believe?" Notice the difference in how you feel.

- **Examine one corrupted area of your life**: Identify where you're experiencing dysfunction (relationships, career, health, finances). Trace it back to discover what value you're prioritizing above truth in that domain.

- **Question your deepest assumptions**: Religious beliefs, political ideologies, spiritual practices—don't defend them. Genuinely ask: "Is this actually true, or do I believe this because it serves other needs (comfort, belonging, identity)?"

- **Be willing to face painful truths**: Real growth requires accepting truths that don't feel good—about yourself, your relationships, your life choices. Notice where you resist or rationalize to avoid discomfort.

- **Choose integrity-aligned work and relationships**: Even if it costs you money, success, or attention, prioritize environments and people that support truth-seeking over those that require you to be inauthentic or defend falsehoods.

- **Develop epistemic rigor**: Learn to evaluate the quality of information sources and claims. Don't accept ideas because they come from friends, feel good, or align with your tribe. Ask: "How do they know this? What's the evidence?"
